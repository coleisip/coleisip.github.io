Most Interesting Man
Re-launch

Brand Event PR Strategy Content Production Talent Integration
The Challenge

After years away, Dos Equis was bringing back its most iconic campaign asset: the Most Interesting Man in the World. The re-launch needed to make noise at scale, introduce a new face for the character, and reassert the brand's cultural relevance — all in a single moment.

The Role

Account lead responsible for concepting and producing a proprietary brand event during the College Football National Championship — one of the highest-profile sports and cultural tentpoles of the year.

Responsibilities
Account Lead Event Concept Production Oversight Talent Strategy PR Integration
Execution

The strategy was built on a simple premise: a press release doesn't move culture, but a room full of the right people does. We engineered a flagship brand event from the ground up — not as a celebration, but as a content and earned media engine designed to generate coverage that would extend the campaign far beyond the night itself.

The anchor was talent: Tom Brady, Dwyane Wade, and Fuerza Regida — a combination that bridged sports, celebrity, and music in a way that gave every major outlet a story. The College Football National Championship provided the cultural context: maximum audience, maximum attention, a moment where sports and entertainment converge at their most amplified.

Every element of the event — from its design and programming to its photo moments and media access — was engineered to generate photography, footage, and press material that fueled a full-scale PR push. The result was an event that felt organic and celebratory to guests, but was rigorously built as a distribution vehicle for the brand's return.

Results

Impressions

Within 72 Hours
