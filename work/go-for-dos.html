"Go For Dos"

Experiential Sponsorship Live Events Broadcast Integration Silver Clio
The Challenge

College football is one of the most competitive sponsorship environments in sports. Brands fight for visibility in a crowded Saturday landscape where fans are loyal to the game — not the advertisers. Dos Equis needed to do more than show up. It needed to own something.

The Role

Client lead for Amplified's role in the Go For Dos campaign — responsible for the on-the-ground experiential program, including the national Roadshow Tour and regional gameday activations across five schools and 28 matchups throughout the 2024–25 season. Credited individually for the Silver Clio.

Responsibilities
Client Lead Experiential Strategy Tour Execution Sponsorship Negotiation Multi-Agency Management
Execution

The campaign's strategic anchor was a first-of-its-kind partnership with Disney and ESPN, making Dos Equis the inaugural sponsor of the two-point conversion — college football's boldest play. Every attempt triggered branded scoreboard graphics, live broadcast mentions, and Chris Fowler-led segments that blurred the line between editorial and advertising. Drew Brees served as campaign spokesperson, driving earned media and fueling a national sweepstakes offering fans up to one million free beers.

On the ground, Amplified executed a five-stop National Roadshow Tour targeting 21+ college students — launching each weekend with Friday night bar takeovers headlined by celebrity DJ duos (Nina Sky, Vavo & Disco Fries), followed by immersive Saturday tailgate activations at ESPN College GameDay and FOX Big Noon Kickoff stops.

Tour stops: Notre Dame @ Texas A&M · Tennessee @ Oklahoma · Kansas @ Arizona State · Colorado @ Texas Tech · USC @ UCLA. Each stop was built as a self-contained content and engagement engine — from sample distribution and lead capture to live social coverage and influencer integration.

The $2M in marketing and production value secured through negotiation ensured the program punched far above its budget, with media integration, broadcast features, and on-site production costs structured to maximize return at every touchpoint.
