About

Marketing at the intersection of brand, culture, and business.

I started my career at a boutique experiential agency as one of eight employees, and spent nearly four years growing into the room — from Partnerships Associate to Senior Manager, taking on increasingly complex programs, bigger budgets, and more senior client relationships along the way.

That trajectory wasn't accidental. I've consistently sought out the hardest problems, the most ambitious briefs, and the opportunities to lead — whether that meant negotiating seven-figure festival partnerships, managing multi-agency campaign ecosystems, or putting a brand on stage at one of the most culturally significant concerts of the year.

Along the way I've had unusual early access: to marquee properties like Coachella, Lollapalooza, and the Miami Open; to campaigns that generated billions of earned media impressions; to clients who trusted me to represent their brand at the highest levels. That exposure shaped how I think about marketing — not as a series of executions, but as an integrated system where brand, culture, and business strategy converge.

I'm now looking to bring that foundation somewhere I can work on the whole picture — partnering across brand, growth, creative, and strategy to build campaigns that don't just show up in culture, but shape it.
